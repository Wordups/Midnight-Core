Everything you need to go from signup to your first finding. Each guide reflects the current product.

Getting started

Midnight turns scattered compliance inputs — policies, framework requirements, findings, and drafts — into a working program.

  1. Sign up with your work email and company name on the login page.
  2. Complete the short onboarding wizard: choose your frameworks (SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO 27001, and more), your primary objective, and how you want to build.
  3. You land on the dashboard. The overview shows policies processed, open gaps, framework coverage, and recent activity — all live for your workspace.
Your workspace is tenant-isolated. Only members of your organization can see your documents and findings.

Uploading documents to Bird Eye

Bird Eye is Midnight's autonomous document review. Give it your existing policies and it indexes and analyzes them.

  1. Open the Migrate Document or Bird Eye surface from the sidebar.
  2. Upload a .docx, .txt, or .md policy document.
  3. Midnight extracts the structure (sections, controls, revision history) and adds it to your policy library.

Running a Bird Eye analysis

Bird Eye scans your library for duplicates, conflicts, stale governance, orphaned procedures, and framework gaps.

  1. Go to the Overview and find the Bird Eye Review panel.
  2. Click Run Bird Eye Review. Midnight reviews every document in your library.
  3. Findings appear by severity. Use Export PDF to download a findings report with the Midnight header and date.
Gap Analysis (sidebar) shows framework coverage and open control gaps, also exportable as a PDF.

Creating a policy

When a gap needs a new document, Midnight drafts one mapped to the relevant control.

  1. Open Create Policy from the sidebar, or chat with Bird Talk, the policy assistant.
  2. Provide the basics — document type, frameworks, and what you know. Midnight handles structure, formatting, and framework mapping.
  3. Review the draft, then download it. Every output is a draft for your review — Midnight prepares, it does not certify.

Inviting a team member

Bring a colleague into your workspace so they can collaborate on your program.

  1. Open Profile & Settings.
  2. Enter your teammate's email in the invite field and send the invitation.
  3. They receive an email link; accepting it provisions their account inside your organization.
Expanded role-based collaboration (analyst / SME task assignment) is rolling out — see the dashboard for the latest.
